2 Second Normal Form (2NF)
Rule: Must be in 1NF + No partial dependency (non-key attribute must depend on the FULL composite key).

Partial Dependencies Found:

acc_id → location, vehicle_id, severity, hospital_name, beds(depends ONLY on acc_id)
staff_name → staff_role(depends ONLY on staff_name)
equip_name → equip_qty(depends ONLY on equip_name)
Solution: Decompose into separate tables, each with a single-column primary key.

3 Third Normal Form (3NF)
Rule: Must be in 2NF + No transitive dependency (non-key attribute must not depend on another non-key attribute).

Transitive Dependencies Found:

ambulance_id → driver_name → driver_contact(driver_contact depends on driver_name, NOT on ambulance_id — this is transitive)
Solution: Remove driver_name and driver_contact from Ambulances table and create a separate Drivers table.

5 Fifth Normal Form (5NF)
Rule: Must have no Join Dependencies. A ternary relationship cannot be split into just two tables without creating spurious (fake) tuples.

Problem: 3-Way Join Dependency — Dispatcher, Hospital, and Vendor
